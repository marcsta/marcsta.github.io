FOUNDER

MALCOLM JENKINS

An 11-year NFL veteran, two-time Super Bowl champion, three-time Pro-Bowl honoree, Malcolm Jenkins is a Safety for the New Orleans Saints, a multiple-year team captain, NFLPA Executive Committee Board member, published author and new Contributor to CNN.

Jenkins experience in front of the camera includes being an NFL guest analyst for Showtime, HBO Now, ESPN and Amazon and as an actor/guest on HBO’s BALLERS, THE DAILY SHOW, THE SHOP among others. In June 2020, CNN hired Jenkins as a regular contributor to the network’s studio shows and special programming. For the past several years, Jenkins has written first-person pieces for the New York Times, Washington Post, CNN and Philadelphia Inquirer among others.

A businessman, Jenkins created Listen Up Media, a production company with the mission to showcase and distribute content that creates social awareness around systemic issues in society and owns Damari Savile, a storefront in Washington Square featuring a collection of custom clothing including ready-to-wear and made-to-measure men’s suits.

A three-time NFL Walter Payton Man of the Year finalist and 2017 NFLPA Byron “Whizzer” White Award winner, Jenkins strives to make a positive difference in the lives of youth in underserved communities through The Malcolm Jenkins Foundation and minorities through the Players Coalition, a 501(c)(3) and 501(c)(4) non-profit led by NFL players dedicated to impacting racial and social inequality.

Football

Jenkins earned a Super Bowl ring with the Philadelphia Eagles in 2018, and as a rookie with the New Orleans Saints in 2010 after being originally selected by the Saints with the 14th-overall pick in the 2009 NFL Draft. Malcolm joined the Eagles in 2014 and lauded Pro Bowl honors with the Eagles in 2015, 2017 and most recently, in 2018. Jenkins rejoined the Saints in 2020 after six seasons with the Eagles. As a team Captain the past several years, his impact on the field has garnered multiple awards, including team leader for most solo tackles (2017, 2019), his fourth NFC Defensive Player of the Week award (2016), in his 100th career start, became and remains the only player in Eagles history to register three consecutive seasons with an INT-TD, and this past season was one of only three NFL players on the field for all of his team’s defensive snaps the entire season. Jenkins is the only NFL safety to start every game since 2014.

At Ohio State University, Jenkins was a team captain and consensus All-America selection as a senior in 2008, and was honored with the Jim Thorpe Award, which is given to the nation’s top defensive back.

Contributor / Broadcasting

Jenkins’ experience in front of the camera includes being an NFL guest analyst for Showtime, HBO Now, ESPN and Amazon and as an actor/guest on HBO’s BALLERS, THE DAILY SHOW, THE SHOP among others. In June 2020, CNN hired Jenkins as a regular contributor to the network’s studio shows and special programming. For the past several years, Jenkins has written first-person pieces on social and racial injustice for the New York Times, Washington Post, CNN and Philadelphia Inquirer among others.

Business

Jenkins has fully immersed himself in the world of fashion.

In 2017, Jenkins launched Damari Savile, a collection of custom clothing including ready-to-wear and made-to-measure men’s suits. Damari Savile has its storefront location in the heart of Philadelphia in Washington Square.

In 2018, Malcolm created Listen Up Media, a production company with the mission to showcase and distribute content that creates social awareness around systemic issues in society, drives meaningful conversation and results in a call to action.  Listen Up Media has several projects in development including sketch comedy and scripted series, documentaries, and digital content.

Philanthropy

Jenkins has inspired many through his actions on and off the field through his three-time Pro Bowl honors, 2019 Walter Payton Man of the Year nomination (his third) and 2017 NFLPA Byron “Whizzer” White Award for extraordinary philanthropic and on-field performance.

Aside from football, Jenkins strives to make a positive difference in the lives of youth in underserved communities through The Malcolm Jenkins Foundation, a non-profit founded by Jenkins and committed to programs, educational initiatives and scholarship opportunities aimed at building character, promoting health and wellness, and helping youth develop the skills needed to succeed in life and become contributing members of their communities. The Foundation is active in New Jersey, Ohio, New Orleans and Philadelphia.

Jenkins also is very engaged in social issues. In February 2017, he co-founded the Players Coalition with retired NFL wide receiver Anquan Boldin to organize a group of NFL players’ fight for racial and social equality. The Players Coalition, a 501(c)(3) and 501(c)(4) non-profit, work is prioritized into three areas of focus: criminal justice reform, police/community relations and education/economic advancement for low-income communities, primarily communities of color. Jenkins has visited prisons, met with police and legislators across the country, held town halls and led players’ advocacy efforts for cash bail, re-entry and parole/probation.
